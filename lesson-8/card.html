Squirrel

January 21 is National Squirrel Appreciation Day! In Greek, the word “squirrel” means “shadow tail”. Babies are called kittens (or kits) and are born blind.

  • Scientific name: Sciuridae
  • Genera & species: 21 genera, more than 200 species
  • Lifespan: 15-18 years
  • Size (length): Ranges greatly by species, from 3-36 inches (7-91 cm)
  • Habitat: Wooded areas, on every continent except Antarctica and Australia

The Sciuridae family is highly varied with 285 species, including the nifty (and cute) Japanese flying squirrel (Pteromys momonga). These clever animals were purposely introduced to US city parks to create “a bucolic atmosphere”. To prevent teeth wear from gnawing on nuts, their front four continue to grow throughout their lifetime. When threatened, squirrels run away in a zigzag pattern as a strategy to escape predators, but it also leads to discombobulated drivers!
